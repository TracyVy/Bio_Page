Entrepreneur

In the early 2000s, during the dot-com bubble burst, in the early 2000s, the economy made a downturn while unemployment rised. I was one semester shy of obtaining a B.S. in International Business, but I started to look for employment opportunities because I knew if I waited longer, the application pool will grow, making it more difficult to "stand out".

Coincidentally, it was also post-9/11. People were traveling less and "staycation", or "self-care", became a thing. I took the opportunity of taking over a failing travel agency business and convert it into a brick and mortar salon in the coveted St. Francis Wood neighborhood in San Francisco. I was bless with instant success.

I spent the next ten years performing demographic and location analysis and managed projects to the open six more locations - five in the Bay Area and one in La Jolla, CA.
