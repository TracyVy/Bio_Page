Entrepreneur

In the early 2000s recession and one semester shy of obtaining my B.S. in International Business, the economy made a downturn, partly due to the 9/11 attack. Employment was low, therefore I knew I had to be creative to be employed.

People were traveling less, and "staycation" or "self-care" became a thing. I took the opportunity of taking over a failing travel agency business and convert it into a brick and mortar salon in the coveted St. Francis Wood neighborhood in San Francisco. I was bless with instant success.

I spent the next ten years performing demographic and location analysis and opened six more locations - five in the Bay Area and one in La Jolla, CA.
